- **Discourse connectives**
  - 发表日期：2025-12-11 | 推荐度：0.801 | 来源：Crossref
  - DOI：10.1093/9780198951094.003.0010
  - 链接：https://doi.org/10.1093/9780198951094.003.0010
  - 摘要：<jats:title>Abstract</jats:title>                   <jats:p>Discourse connectives are words or fixed expressions that express a discourse relation between the host and the preceding or following context, such as by the way, however, on the other hand, and so on, and so on. Importantly, the relations they express are not between sentences but rather between Discourse Units, which can be either sentences or hierarchical assemblies of sentence meanings. Many discourse connectives show no evidence of a syntactic category. Connectives vary somewhat unsystematically in their degree of freedom of position; for instance, by the way is extremely free, while so is restricted to initial position. Overall, then, discourse connectives behave asyntactically, like the parenthetical constructions of previous chapters. The chapter formalizes a representative collection of discourse connectives in Parallel Architectural terms: by the way, therefore, for example, in general, first, finally, too, however.</jats:p>
