- **Objectivity and the Space of Reasons**
  - 发表日期：2026-01-17 | 推荐度：0.806 | 来源：Crossref
  - DOI：10.1093/oso/9780197745083.003.0011
  - 链接：https://doi.org/10.1093/oso/9780197745083.003.0011
  - 摘要：<jats:title>Abstract</jats:title>                   <jats:p>This chapter explores the relation between two capacities whose development enables the kind of self-conscious thinking that we humans are capable of: (1) we can assess our judgments with a view to whether they get things right; (2) we can assess them also with a view to reasons for or against them. A common view seems to be that (1) is more basic and makes (2) possible. Michael Tomasello’s account of their ontogeny seems to lean toward this view. The chapter articulates three considerations that, taken together, suggests a more symmetrical account: that any grip on thoughts as objectively correct always already involves some grip on them as located in a space of reasons. On this view, a basic grip on both correctness and reasons is embodied in discursive abilities, and both of these grips co-develop from simpler, prereflective forms toward increasingly complex, reflective forms of understanding.</jats:p>
